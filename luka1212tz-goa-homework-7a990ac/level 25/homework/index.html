about lions

Lions are most active at night and live in a variety of habitats but prefer grassland, savanna, dense scrub,
and open woodland. Historically, they ranged across much of Europe, Asia, and Africa, but now
they are found mainly in parts of Africa south of the Sahara

  • fun facts:
  • THEY HUNT DURING STORMS
  • NEARLY ALL WILD LIONS LIVE IN AFRICA

zebras

The plains zebras can weigh up to 850 pounds.
Their territory stretches from southern Ethiopia and South Sudan to northern South Africa.
The smallest zebra species, the mountain zebra, can still weigh up to 800 pounds.
They are found only in South Africa, Namibia, and Angola.

  • fun facts:
  • Every zebra has a unique stripe pattern
  • Zebras can sleep standing up

tigers

Tigers have soft toe pads which help them walk silently through their habitat.
A tiger will typically travel 6-12 miles during a night's hunting.
During the day, a tiger's stripes can act as camouflage,
allowing it to blend in with its surroundings for stalking and ambushing its prey

  • fun facts:
  • Tigers are the largest among other wild cats
  • Tigers love to swim and play in the water
